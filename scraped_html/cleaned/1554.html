## Answer

The postorder traversal sequence of the given binary search tree is **15, 10, 23, 25, 20, 35, 42, 39, 30** (Option D).

---

### Explanation

To find the postorder traversal of a Binary Search Tree (BST) when only the **preorder** is given, we use the fact that the **inorder traversal** of a BST is always the sorted sequence of its elements.

1. **Identify the Traversals:**
* **Preorder (Given):**

2. **Construct the Tree:**
* **Root:** The first element in preorder is the root: **30**.
* **Left Subtree:** Elements in inorder appearing before 30 are .
* **Right Subtree:** Elements in inorder appearing after 30 are .
* Repeat this recursively:
* In the left subtree, **20** is the root (first in preorder). Its left is  and right is .
* In the right subtree, **39** is the root. Its left is **35** and right is **42**.




3. **Determine Postorder (Left  Right  Root):**
* Postorder of  with root 10: **15, 10**
* Postorder of  with root 25: **23, 25**
* Postorder of left branch (root 20): **15, 10, 23, 25, 20**
* Postorder of right branch (root 39): **35, 42, 39**
* **Final Sequence:** **15, 10, 23, 25, 20, 35, 42, 39, 30**
