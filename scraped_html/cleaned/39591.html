Consider the following two statements:

  1. If all states of an NFA are accepting states then the language accepted by the NFA is $\Sigma_{}^{*}$.
  2. There exists a regular language $A$ such that for all languages $B$, $A \cap B$ is regular.

Which one of the following is CORRECT ?

  1. Only I is true
  2. Only II is true
  3. Both I and II are true
  4. Both I and II are false

EXPLAINATION FOR STATEMENT 1 BEING FALSE.

Yes, the core reason the NFA doesn't accept all of Σ* — despite all of its states being accepting — is because of the existence of dead (non-reachable or trap) states in the corresponding DFA . Let’s go deeper into this:

Let’s revisit the NFA:

  • States : {q0}

  • Start state : q0

  • Accepting states : q0

  • Alphabet : {a, b}

  • Transitions :

    • q0 --a--> q0

    • no transition on b

This NFA accepts only strings in a* , not strings like b , ab , aab , etc.

🔴 Even if all NFA states are accepting , the language ≠ Σ*
Because after determinization, some strings may land in a trap state , due to missing transitions in NFA .
