A binary search tree is generated by inserting in order the following integers:

$$50, 15, 62, 5, 20, 58, 91, 3, 8, 37, 60, 24$$

The number of nodes in the left subtree and right subtree of the root respectively is

  1. $(4, 7)$
  2. $(7, 4)$
  3. $(8, 3)$
  4. $(3, 8)$

Correct Option: B

Root will be $50$. now insert one by one, greater to $50$ in the right sub tree, lesser in left sub tree.

Or you can simply count the number looking at the i/p. less than $50$ are $7$. more than $50$ are $4$.

you are doing a pre-order traversal of a list of numbers. How did you do that? That is not possible.

Pre-order, Post-order, and in-order traversal are defined on trees only.

An in-order traversal of BST gives a sorted list if that is what you are referring to.
