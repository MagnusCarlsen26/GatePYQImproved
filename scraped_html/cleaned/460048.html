GATE CSE 2025 | Set 1 | Question: 32

Arjun asked Feb 27, 2025 • edited Mar 23, 2025 by jothee_new
3,417 views
12 12 votes

Consider the following four variable Boolean function in sum-of-product form

$$ F\left(b_{3}, b_{2}, b_{1}, b_{0}\right)=\sum(0,2,4,8,10,11,12)$$

where the value of the function is computed by considering $b_{3} b_{2} b_{1} b_{0}$ as a $4$-bit binary number, where $b_{3}$ denotes the most significant bit and $b_{0}$ denotes the least significant bit. Note that there are no don't care terms. Which ONE of the following options is the CORRECT minimized Boolean expression for $F$ ?

  1. $\bar{b}_{1} \bar{b}_{0}+\bar{b}_{2} \bar{b}_{0}+b_{1} \bar{b}_{2} b_{3}$
  2. $\bar{b}_{1} \bar{b}_{0}+\bar{b}_{2} \bar{b}_{0}$
  3. $\bar{b}_{2} \bar{b}_{0}+b_{1} b_{2} b_{3}$
  4. $\bar{b}_{0} \bar{b}_{2}+\bar{b}_{3}$

Kmap for the given boolean function:

$$
\begin{array}{c}
F(b_3,b_2,b_1,b_0) = \sum(0,2,4,8,10,11,12) \\
\\
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
& 00 & 01 & 11 & 10 \\
\hline
00 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 1 \\
\hline
01 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
\hline
11 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
\hline
10 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \\
\hline
\end{array}
\\[0.5em]
\end{array}
$$

Creating Maximal Cube for minimal SOP:

$$
\boxed{
\begin{array}{c}
\textbf{Karnaugh Map 1: Maximal Group : } \overline{b}_1\,\overline{b}_0 \\
\\
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
& 00 & 01 & 11 & 10 \\
\hline
00 & \color{gold}{1} & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
\hline
01 & \color{gold}{1} & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
\hline
11 & \color{gold}{1} & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
\hline
10 & \color{gold}{1} & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
\hline
\end{array}
\\[1em]
\text{This group covers minterms } 0,4,8,12 \text{ (column } b_1b_0 = 00). \\
\text{Since } b_1 = 0 \text{ and } b_0 = 0 \text{ are fixed, and } b_3,b_2 \text{ vary,} \\
\text{the product term is } \boxed{\overline{b}_1\,\overline{b}_0}.
\end{array}
}
$$

$$
\boxed{
\begin{array}{c}
\textbf{Karnaugh Map 2: Maximal Group : } \overline{b}_2\,\overline{b}_0 \\
\\
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
& 00 & 01 & 11 & 10 \\
\hline
00 & \color{lime}{1} & 0 & 0 & \color{lime}{1} \\
\hline
01 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
\hline
11 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
\hline
10 & \color{lime}{1} & 0 & 0 & \color{lime}{1} \\
\hline
\end{array}
\\[1em]
\text{This } 2\times2 \text{ block covers minterms } 0,2,8,10. \\
\text{Here, } b_2 = 0 \text{ and } b_0 = 0 \text{ are constant; } b_3,b_1 \text{ vary.} \\
\text{The resulting term is } \boxed{\overline{b}_2\,\overline{b}_0}.
\end{array}
}
$$

$$
\boxed{
\begin{array}{c}
\textbf{Karnaugh Map 3: Maximal Group :  } b_1\,\overline{b}_2\,b_3 \\
\\
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
& 00 & 01 & 11 & 10 \\
\hline
00 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
\hline
01 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
\hline
11 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
\hline
10 & 0 & 0 & \color{orange}{1} & \color{orange}{1} \\
\hline
\end{array}
\\[1em]
\text{This pair covers minterms } 10 \text{ and } 11. \\
\text{Fixed: } b_3 = 1,\; b_2 = 0,\; b_1 = 1;\; b_0 \text{ varies.} \\
\text{Thus, the implicant is } \boxed{b_1\,\overline{b}_2\,b_3}.
\end{array}
}
$$

Final Minimized Expression

The union of the three maximal prime implicants covers all required minterms:

  • $\overline{b}_1\,\overline{b}_0$ → 0, 4, 8, 12
  • $\overline{b}_2\,\overline{b}_0$ → 0, 2, 8, 10
  • $b_1\,\overline{b}_2\,b_3$ → 10, 11

All minterms in $\{0,2,4,8,10,11,12\}$ are covered with no missing or extra terms.

Hence, the minimal sum-of-products form is:

$$
\color{skyblue} \boxed{\text{A.} F = \overline{b}_1\,\overline{b}_0 + \overline{b}_2\,\overline{b}_0 + b_1\,\overline{b}_2\,b_3}
$$
