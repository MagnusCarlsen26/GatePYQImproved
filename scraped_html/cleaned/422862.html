Let us rename the Person relation into 3 different relations

$\rho R(Person)$
$\rho S(Person)$

$\rho P(Person)$

$R1 \leftarrow \sigma R.city = S.city \wedge R.pid \neq S.pid (R \times S)$

$R2 \leftarrow \sigma R.city = P.city \wedge R.pid \neq P.pid (R1 \times P)$

$R3 \leftarrow \prod city(R2)$

Hence ,we need 3 tables and 2 cross products.

We might need to join the Person relation with itself multiple times, each time ensuring that the city is the same but the pids are different.

\[
\rho_{R}(\text{Person}) \quad \rho_{S}(\text{Person}) \quad \rho_{P}(\text{Person})
\]
\[
R1 \leftarrow \sigma_{\substack{R.\text{city} = S.\text{city} \\ \land \; R.\text{pid} \neq S.\text{pid}}}(R \times S) \quad \text{(1 cross product)}
\]
\[
R2 \leftarrow \sigma_{\substack{R.\text{city} = P.\text{city} \\ \land \; R.\text{pid} \neq P.\text{pid} \\ \land \; S.\text{pid} \neq P.\text{pid}}}(R1 \times P) \quad \text{(2 cross products total)}
\]
\[
R3 \leftarrow \pi_{\text{city}}(R2)
\]

So 2
