Consider a complete undirected graph with vertex set $\{0, 1, 2, 3, 4\}$. Entry $W_{ij}$ in the matrix $W$ below is the weight of the edge $\{i, j\}$

$$W=\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 & 8 & 1 & 4 \\ 1 & 0 & 12 & 4 & 9 \\ 8 & 12 & 0 & 7 & 3 \\ 1 & 4 & 7 & 0 & 2 \\ 4 & 9 & 3 & 2 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$$

What is the minimum possible weight of a path $P$ from vertex $1$ to vertex $2$ in this graph such that $P$ contains at most $3$ edges?

  1. $7$
  2. $8$
  3. $9$
  4. $10$

Normal approach and I think good one also and this ensures you get no error and you can apply fastly also.

Now, you have to notice vertex 1 to vertex 2 visit needed at most 3 edges and this means at most 2 vertices.

Exactly 1 vertex(2 edges) between 1 and 2:

Scenario:  1  _   2    (Now this gap can be filled with any of 0, 3 , 4 )

So,   1 0 2  , cost= 1+8 = 9

1 3 2 ,  cost = 4+7 = 11

1 4 2 , cost = 9+ …..   (stop don’t go further you already know 9 is smallest till now.)

Exactly 2 vertices(3 edges) between 1 and 2 :

Scenario:   1  _  _  2  (Now this can be filled by choosing any 2 from {0,3,4}.So, $3P_{2}$ = 6 ways)

Possibilities :

1 0 3 2 ,   cost =1+1+7= 9

1 3 0 2 ,  cost = 4+1+8= 13

1 0 4 2 ,   cost = 1+4+3= 8 (minimum among also)

1 4 0 2 ,  cost = 9+ …..   (stop because you find minimum =8 already)

1 3 4 2 ,   cost =4+2+3=9

1 4 3 2 ,  cost = 9+…..  (stop reason same as 1 4 0 2) 

 Note : why not nCr here because position matters here.
