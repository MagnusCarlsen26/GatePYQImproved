A schedule of three database transactions $T_{1}, T_{2}$, and $T_{3}$ is shown. $R_{i}(A)$ and $W_{i}(A)$ denote read and write of data item $A$ by transaction $T_{i}, i=1,2,3$. The transaction $T_{1}$ aborts at the end. Which other transaction(s) will be required to be rolled back?
$$ R_{1}(X) W_{1}(Y) R_{2}(X) R_{2}(Y) R_{3}(Y) \operatorname{ABORT}\left(T_{1}\right) $$

  1. Only $T_{2}$
  2. Only $T_{3}$
  3. Both $T_{2}$ and $T_{3}$
  4. Neither $T_{2}$ nor $T_{3}$

Explanation :-

The given schedule is:

  • T₁ : R₁(X) , W₁(Y)

  • T₂ : R₂(X) , R₂(Y)

  • T₃ : R₃(Y)

  • ABORT(T₁)

Analyze the read–write dependencies :

  • T₁ writes to Y via W₁(Y) .

  • T₂ reads Y via R₂(Y) after T₁'s write.

  • T₃ reads Y via R₃(Y) after T₁'s write.

  • T₂ reads Y after T₁ wrote it → T₂ is dependent on T₁ .

  • T₃ reads Y after T₁ wrote it → T₃ is dependent on T₁ .

Both T₂ and T₃ require rollback due to cascading abort (Rollback) caused by T₁'s abort.

Ans :- C

If a transaction $T_j$ reads a data item written by another transaction $T_i$ before $T_i$ commits, and $T_i$ later aborts, then $T_j$ has performed a dirty read . To preserve consistency, $T_j$ must also be rolled back this is known as cascading rollback .

$$
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|}
\hline
T_1 & T_2 & T_3 \\
\hline
R_1(X) & & \\
\hline
W_1(Y) & & \\
\hline
& R_2(X) & \\
\hline
& R_2(Y) & \\
\hline
& & R_3(Y) \\
\hline
\text{ABORT}(T_1) & & \\
\hline
\end{array}
$$

Dependency Analysis

  • $T_1$ writes to $Y$: $W_1(Y)$
  • $T_2$ subsequently reads $Y$: $R_2(Y)$ → dirty read from $T_1$
  • $T_3$ subsequently reads $Y$: $R_3(Y)$ → dirty read from $T_1$

Since $T_1$ aborts, both $T_2$ and $T_3$ have consumed uncommitted (and now invalid) data. Therefore, to maintain database consistency, both must be rolled back.

The abort of $T_1$ necessitates the rollback of all transactions that read its uncommitted writes. Hence, the correct choice is:

$$\color{lime}
\boxed{\text{C. Both } T_2 \text{ and } T_3}$$
