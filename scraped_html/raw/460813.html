Booth’s multiplication algorithm is an efficient method to multiply two signed binary numbers represented in two’s complement form. It reduces the number of addition and subtraction operations by examining pairs of adjacent bits in the multiplier (including an implicit 0 appended to the least significant side).

For each bit position $i$ of the multiplier $Q$, the algorithm examines the pair $(q_i, q_{i-1})$. Based on this pair, one of the following actions is taken:

  • If $(q_i, q_{i-1}) = (0,0)$ or $(1,1)$:  
    • No change in value; no arithmetic operation is needed.  
    • Recoded digit $d_i = 0$

If $(q_i, q_{i-1}) = (0,1)$:  
  → This signals the start of a block of 1s; we add the multiplicand.  
  → Recoded digit $d_i = +1$

If $(q_i, q_{i-1}) = (1,0)$:  
  → This signals the end of a block of 1s; we subtract the multiplicand.  
  → Recoded digit $d_i = -1$

Each non-zero recoded digit ($+1$ or $-1$) corresponds to one addition or subtraction of the multiplicand (possibly shifted), which is then accumulated over the algorithm’s iterations. Therefore, the total number of addition and subtraction operations equals the number of non-zero digits in the Booth recoding of the multiplier.

Given Multiplier

The multiplier is:

$$
Q = 1010010010101010_2
$$

This is a 16-bit signed number in two’s complement. To apply Booth’s algorithm, we append a 0 to the right as $q_{-1}$:

$$
q_{15}\, q_{14}\, \dots\, q_1\, q_0\, q_{-1} = 1\ 0\ 1\ 0\ 0\ 1\ 0\ 0\ 1\ 0\ 1\ 0\ 1\ 0\ 1\ 0\ 0
$$

Now we examine each pair $(q_i, q_{i-1})$ for $i = 0$ to $15$ and determine the recoded digit $d_i$.

Booth Recoding Table

$$
\begin{array}{c|c|c|c}
i & q_i & q_{i-1} & d_i \\
\hline
0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
1 & 1 & 0 & -1 \\
2 & 0 & 1 & +1 \\
3 & 1 & 0 & -1 \\
4 & 0 & 1 & +1 \\
5 & 1 & 0 & -1 \\
6 & 0 & 1 & +1 \\
7 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
8 & 1 & 0 & -1 \\
9 & 0 & 1 & +1 \\
10 & 1 & 0 & -1 \\
11 & 0 & 1 & +1 \\
12 & 1 & 0 & -1 \\
13 & 0 & 1 & +1 \\
14 & 1 & 0 & -1 \\
15 & 1 & 1 & 0 \\
\end{array}
$$

Count of Arithmetic Operations

We count the number of non-zero $d_i$ values:

  • Non-zero recoded digits occur at indices:  $i = 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14$
  • Total = 13 non-zero digits

Each corresponds to one addition ($+1$) or subtraction ($-1$).

$$
\color{lime} \boxed{Answer: 13}
$$
